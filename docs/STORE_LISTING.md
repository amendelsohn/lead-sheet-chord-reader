## Long description

LeadSheet Chord Reader turns busy chord-sheet pages on popular online tab and chord sites into a clean, focused reader — the way you actually want to read a song while you play.

Open any supported chord page and click the pick icon in the bottom-right corner. Your song pops into a distraction-free overlay: just the chords and lyrics, spaced and sized for playing.

Core features:

- Transpose up or down with key-aware accidentals. The reader tracks the song's key and shows the current key next to the transpose counter. A sharps/flats toggle flips accidental display even at transpose 0.
- Roman numeral and Nashville number display. Show chord scale degrees alongside or instead of chord names, with automatic key inference for songs that don't declare one.
- Auto-scroll with adjustable speed. Works vertically for single-column reading, or horizontally when you're in paged layout.
- Two layouts: Vertical (one column, scroll down) or Pages (fills each column top-to-bottom, scroll right to reveal more).
- Light and dark themes. Defaults to your system preference.
- Font-size control, keyboard shortcuts for everything, and a responsive toolbar that collapses into a menu on narrow windows.
- Print-friendly. Press Ctrl+P and get a clean, playable chord sheet — no site chrome, no ads.

Privacy and safety:

- No tracking, no analytics, no accounts.
- Works offline. The reader parses the page you already loaded — it doesn't scrape, fetch, or phone home.
- The reader UI lives in a Shadow DOM, so its styles never touch the host page.

Open source (MIT). Not affiliated with or endorsed by any of the sites it renders content from; all site names and logos belong to their respective owners.

# Firefox Add-ons (AMO)

## Summary

## Long description

LeadSheet Chord Reader is a focused reading layer for chord-sheet pages on popular online tab and chord sites. It takes the chords and lyrics that are already on the page and re-renders them in a clean, playable overlay — no ads, no sidebars, no visual noise between you and the song.

What it does

- Transpose up or down with key-aware accidentals. The reader detects the song's key, shows it next to the transpose counter, and spells chords correctly as you move around the circle of fifths. A sharps-vs-flats toggle lets you flip the accidental style even at transpose 0.
- Roman numeral and Nashville number display. Show scale-degree labels alongside or instead of chord names. Works even when a song doesn't declare its key — the extension infers one from the chords present.
- Auto-scroll with adjustable speed. In vertical layout it scrolls down; in paged layout it scrolls across. Tap space to start and stop.
- Two layouts: Vertical (one column, scroll down) or Pages (columns fill top-to-bottom and you scroll sideways to reveal more — great on wide screens).
- Light and dark themes, a font-size control, and a responsive toolbar that collapses less-used controls into a menu on narrow windows.
- Full keyboard shortcuts for transpose, layout, theme, auto-scroll, font size, and close.
- Print-friendly. Ctrl+P prints a clean chord sheet with no site chrome.

Privacy

- No tracking, no analytics, no accounts, no remote config.
- Works offline. The extension parses the page content client-side — it never scrapes or calls out to the network.
- The reader UI is mounted in a Shadow DOM so its styles can't leak into the host page and the host page's styles can't leak in.

Open source under the MIT license. This project is not affiliated with or endorsed by any of the sites it renders content from; all site names and trademarks belong to their respective owners.
